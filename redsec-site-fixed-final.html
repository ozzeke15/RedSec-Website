Approach

Security tested like an attacker would.

REDSEC is being built around practical offensive security. The focus is not only on identifying vulnerabilities, but on validating how well defensive systems, monitoring pipelines and operational processes perform against realistic attacker behavior.

01 / Pentesting

Controlled offensive assessments

Pentesting focuses on identifying exploitable weaknesses in applications, infrastructure and internal environments. The goal is to provide clear, actionable findings that help prioritize real risk instead of generic noise.

02 / Adversary Simulation

Validation through attacker behavior

Adversary simulation emulates realistic attacker TTPs based on frameworks such as MITRE ATT&CK. The objective is to validate whether security controls, SIEM pipelines, EDR platforms and operational monitoring can properly detect malicious behavior.

03 / Purple Teaming

Collaborative defensive improvement

Purple teaming combines offensive testing with defensive collaboration. Instead of operating entirely in stealth, findings are reviewed together with defenders to improve alerting, visibility and incident response readiness.

Focused on realistic security validation.

Rather than relying on exaggerated marketing or unrealistic “elite hacker” positioning, REDSEC focuses on technically grounded offensive security. The objective is to emulate realistic attacker behavior in a controlled way, helping organizations better understand exposure, improve detection quality and strengthen defensive maturity.
